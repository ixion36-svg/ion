Getting Started

ION (Intelligent Operating Network) is a Security Operations Center platform built for the Guarded Glass team. It connects your Elasticsearch alerts, OpenCTI threat intelligence, TIDE detection rules, and Ollama AI into a single workspace.

1. Log In

Navigate to ION and enter your credentials. Your dashboard will load based on your role.

First login? Your credentials are set by your SOC administrator.

2. Your Dashboard

The dashboard is role-specific. Analysts see alert queues. Leads see team metrics. Engineers see system status.

3. Focus Mode

Have multiple roles? Use the Focus Mode pill buttons on the dashboard to switch between role views.

Focus mode restricts permissions to one role at a time for clarity.

4. Navigation

The top nav has dropdown menus: Knowledge, Analyst, Engineering, DFIR, and Settings. Items show/hide based on your role.

Triage Actions

1
Acknowledge — Claim the alert. Status changes to "acknowledged" and syncs to Elasticsearch.
2
Investigate — Check the alert detail, related alerts, observables, and MITRE context.
3
Escalate or Close — Create a case for investigation, or close with a reason (True Positive, False Positive, Benign).
Triage Suggestions: ION now suggests triage actions based on historical closure data. If a rule was closed as FP 80% of the time, you'll see a suggestion badge. Access via GET /api/triage-suggestions?rule_name=X.

Closure Reasons

True Positive
Confirmed real attack. Document findings.
False Positive
Not a real threat. Consider rule tuning.
Benign True Positive
Real activity but authorized/expected.

Observables (IOC Tracking)

Track IPs, domains, hashes, emails, and URLs across your investigations. Enrich from OpenCTI, track sighting counts, and flag stale IOCs.

Batch Enrichment
Select multiple observables and enrich them all from OpenCTI in one click.
Staleness Tracking
IOCs not re-enriched in 30+ days are flagged. Check /api/ioc-staleness.
Whitelisting
Mark known-good observables to prevent false alerting.
Case Linking
Observables auto-link to cases and alerts where they appear.

Attack Stories

ION automatically correlates alerts by host/user into "attack stories" — multi-step attack narratives with kill chain visualization. No other SIEM does this.

Attack Story: SRV-DC01 (Score: 78/100)
Entity SRV-DC01 was targeted across 4 stages of the kill chain over 2.5 hours. Starting with initial-access, progressing through execution and credential-access, reaching lateral-movement. 5 unique detection rules fired.
recon→ initial-access→ execution→ persistence→ priv-esc→ defense-evasion→ credential-access→ discovery→ lateral-movement

Navigate to /attack-stories and select a time window. Stories are scored 0-100 based on kill chain coverage and alert severity.

Playbooks

Step-by-step response procedures for common scenarios. Execute playbooks during investigations, track step completion, and measure effectiveness.

1
Browse Playbooks — 25+ SOC playbooks across 8 categories (Phishing, Malware, Ransomware, etc.)
2
Start Execution — Link a playbook to a case and work through each step
3
Track Progress — Each step can be marked complete. The playbook tracks overall completion.
4
Effectiveness — Check /api/playbook-analytics to see which playbooks lead to faster case resolution.

Rule Tuning

The Rule Tuning page (/rule-tuning) cross-references TIDE rules with case closure outcomes to identify:

FP-Heavy Rules
Rules closed as false positive > 60% of the time
High-Value Rules
Rules consistently producing true positives
Silent Rules
Enabled in TIDE but never firing — misconfigured?

Key Workflows

Workflow 1: Triage an Alert

1
Go to Alerts
Open /alerts, filter by Open status and severity
↓
2
Review Alert
Click to expand. Check MITRE mapping, host, user, raw data
↓
3
Check Triage Suggestion
ION shows historical closure data for this rule. 80% FP? Probably FP.
↓
4
Acknowledge
Click "Acknowledge" to claim. Add notes about your findings.
↓
?
Decision Point
True Positive? → Create Case → Investigate
False Positive? → Close as FP → Note why

Workflow 2: Investigate a Case

1
Open Case
Go to /cases, select your assigned case
↓
2
Check Entity Timeline
Go to /entity-timeline, search the affected host. See all related events across data sources.
↓
3
Check Similar Cases
ION shows past cases with matching patterns. How were they resolved? What was the MTTR?
↓
4
Run Playbook
Attach a playbook and work through steps. Extract IOCs. Enrich observables.
↓
5
Close Case
Document findings, select closure reason, close. MTTR is automatically tracked.

Generate Report

2
Review Highlights

3
Print or Share
